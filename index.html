DS is All You Need

Abstract: This paper introduces a novel dynamic system (DS) learning framework for trajectory attraction and disturbance robustness in robot control tasks. Specifically, we propose a method for designing trajectory-attractive potential fields on SE(3) spaces, which is based on diffeomorphisms and a newly constructed latent space potential field. This method allows for the generation of stable and disturbance-robust dynamic systems that can adapt to various environmental changes and ensure the attraction of desired trajectories. The core innovation of our approach lies in the use of conditional diffeomorphisms, enabling the system to autonomously generate different dynamic systems depending on environmental observations. Our results demonstrate significant improvements in disturbance rejection, trajectory convergence, and the systemâ€™s ability to generalize to unseen conditions. Through experiments in robotic assembly tasks, including peg-in-hole and bottle cap assembly, as well as dynamic obstacle avoidance, we validate the effectiveness of our approach. This work paves the way for more autonomous and adaptive robot systems capable of responding to diverse real-world conditions.
